Abdullah Quilliam

The man, ideals and legacy

William Henry Quilliam, a local Liverpool solicitor and resident embraced Islam in 1887 (aged 31), after returning from a visit to Morocco, and took on the name Abdullah. He claimed that he was the first native Englishman to embrace Islam.

His conversion led to a remarkable story of the growth of Islam in Victorian Britain. This history is now beginning to emerge and has important lessons for Muslims in Britain and around the world.

Here's a time line of Abdullah Quilliam's life:

  • 1856 - Birth: 10th April, 1856 - William Henry Quilliam was born at 22 Eliot Street in Liverpool. He spent his childhood on the Isle of Man but most of his working life in Liverpool.
  • 1878 - Becomes a solicitor: December, 1878 - Works as a solicitor and criminal lawyer at 28 Church St, Liverpool. The Liverpool Weekly Courier described him as the “unofficial Attorney-General of Liverpool”.
  • 1887 - Liverpool Muslim Institute: September, 1887 - Initially at Mount Vernon Street then 8 Brougham Terrace. Pall Mall Gazette wrote: “The mosque at Liverpool is a place of worship for thousands of Mahommedans”.
  • 1887 - First Convert - Djem Ali Hamilton became his first covert to Islam, followed by Elizabeth Cates who took the name of Fatima after her conversion.
  • 1888 - Public announcement of conversion to Islam - He renounced Christianity at the age of 32, changed his name to Abdullah and announced his conversion in the Liverpool media.
  • 1888 - Providing breakfast for poor children - This led to 200-400 children being fed in the morning on Christmas Day and 400-600 in the evening.
  • 1889 - About 20 English converts - This led to 200-400 children being fed in the morning on Christmas Day and 400-600 in the evening.
  • 1889 - First publication of Faith of Islam: July 1889 - The pamphlet Faith of Islam was first published. The first edition had 2000 copies and a further 3000 copies were published in 1890.
  • 1889 - Number of converts reaches 50 - Among the converts was an Anglican clergyman, Rev H.H.Johnson.
  • 1890 - Protest against play: October, 1890 - The muslim community organised a protest against the play “Mahomet”, by Hall Caine. It is thought that Abdullah Quilliam was instrumental in the halting of this play.
  • 1891 - First public Muslim funeral in Liverpool - Michael Hall a former Methodist preacher who converted to Islam in 1890 was buried in a Liverpool cemetery.
  • 1891 - First public Muslim marriage ceremony in Liverpool: April, 1891 - Picture: Abdullah Quilliam officiates at a Moslem Wedding in Liverpool Mosque from an American newspaper, dated 1903.
  • 1892 - Nember of converts reaches 83 - This was the most successful year for the number of converts, bringing a further 33 into the fold of Islam.
  • 1893 - First edition of the Islamic World: July, 1893 - A monthly journal published by Abdullah Quilliam that had world-wide circulation.
  • 1893 - Gives his first Fatwa - Abdullah Quilliams first fatwa warning Muslims not to fight fellow Muslims in Sudan.
  • 1894 - Sheikh of the British Isles: July, 1894 - The Caliph of Islam, Sultan Abdul Hameed II gave Abdullah Quilliam the official title of Sheikh al-Islam for the British Isles.
  • 1895 - Becomes ill due to Malaria: September, 1895 - An illness which would trouble him for the rest of his life.
  • 1896 - Opens Muslim home for unwanted children - Opened at 12 Broughmam Terrace, called the Medina Home.
  • 1902 - Mosque visit by Shareef Abdul Karim Murad: May 1902 - Shareef Abdul Karim Murad, The Guardian of the sacred mosque in Medina, visited the mosque. Considered one of the most respected leaders in the Muslim world.
  • 1903 - Funeral prayer for Lord Stanley: December, 1903 - Abdullah Quilliam held a funeral prayer (janaza) in memory of Abdur Rahman or Henry Edward John Stanley, the third Baron of Alderley.
  • 1908 - Abdullah Quilliam leaves Liverpool - Leaves for Constantinople never to return to Liverpool. The latter years of his life were spent in the Isle of Man and London.

  • 1932 - Death of Abdullah Quilliam: 28th April, 1932 - On the 28th April Abdullah Quilliam was buried at Brookwood Cemetery, Woking close to other famous Muslim personalities: Lord Headley and Marmaduke Picktall.
